Institutional Background – CHARMIE

CHARMIE was developed by the Laboratory of Automation and Robotics (LAR), a research laboratory integrated in the Department of Industrial Electronics (DEI) at the University of Minho, Portugal.

The LAR research group focuses on robotics, autonomous systems, human-robot interaction, and intelligent control systems.

LAR has been actively participating in RoboCup competitions since 1999. The laboratory competes in multiple RoboCup leagues, including:

- RoboCup Middle Size League (LAR@MSL)
- RoboCup@Home (LAR@Home)
- RoboCup Humanoid League (LAR@Humanoids)

The LAR@Home team focuses on service robotics for domestic and assistive environments.

CHARMIE development started in 2020 as a collaborative home and healthcare assistant robot platform.

RoboCup Participation History (CHARMIE – RoboCup@Home):

- 2023 – RoboCup Bordeaux, France: 7th place (CHARMIE debut)
- 2024 – RoboCup (Eindhoven, Netherlands): 5th place, with significant score improvement
- 2023 – Portugal Open (Tomar, Portugal): 3rd place
- 2024 – Portugal Open (Paredes de Coura, Portugal): 1st place

The LAR team emphasizes:
- Innovation in autonomous navigation
- Human-centered robot design
- Real-world robustness
- Offline-capable systems
- Integration of advanced AI techniques into physical robotics

CHARMIE represents the University of Minho and LAR in international RoboCup competitions and research demonstrations.
